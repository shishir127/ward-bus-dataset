Ward connectivity rankings

Every ward is ranked by a composite connectivity score (0–100) that blends three measures, each converted to a percentile rank across all 367 wards: effective bus reach (50%) — a geometric mean of pedestrian coverage (% of ward within a 5-min / 400 m walk of a bus stop) and office connectivity (% of major employment areas reachable by direct bus), so a ward only scores well if residents can both walk to a stop and those stops serve useful destinations; metro access (30%) — % of ward within a 400 m walk of a Namma Metro station; and terminal access (20%) — % of ward within 800 m of a major BMTC bus terminal. A score of 100 means best-served; the lowest-scoring wards have no walkable bus access to office areas and no metro or terminal nearby. Click any row to jump to that ward on the map.

score = 0.50 × rank(bus reach) + 0.30 × rank(metro) + 0.20 × rank(terminal)  · bus reach = √(PCA 400m % × office direct %)  · rank(x) = percentile across all 367 wards

Transit Hub Connectivity

BMTC routes radiate outward from major bus terminals — wards with no direct terminal connection must always transfer buses to reach the wider network. This report identifies those transfer deserts and flags metro stations with poor onward bus access.

Transfer deserts — wards with 0 direct terminal connections

These wards have no bus route that begins or ends at a major terminal. Residents must change buses for every inter-area journey.

Office Area Bus Reach

For each major employment cluster in Bengaluru, this report shows how many wards are connected by at least one direct bus route — meaning a bus that serves the ward and stops within the office cluster boundary. Clusters at the top are the hardest to reach by bus and are prime candidates for new or extended routes.
